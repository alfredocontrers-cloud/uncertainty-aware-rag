# White Collar Crime

White collar crime refers to non-violent crimes committed for financial gain, typically by individuals or organizations in positions of trust.

## Common Types of White Collar Crime

- Fraud: Deceptive practices used to gain financial advantage
- Insider trading: Trading securities using material, non-public information
- Bribery: Offering or receiving something of value to influence decisions
- Embezzlement: Misappropriation of funds entrusted to someone

## Insider Trading

Insider trading occurs when an individual trades securities based on material, non-public information. This undermines market fairness and creates an uneven playing field for investors.

## Consequences

White collar crime can damage market integrity, reduce investor confidence, and lead to significant financial losses.

If not properly regulated, activities like insider trading can distort markets and erode trust in financial systems.
