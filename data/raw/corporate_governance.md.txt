# Corporate Governance

Corporate governance refers to the system of rules, practices, and processes by which a company is directed and controlled.

## Role of the Board of Directors

The board of directors is responsible for overseeing the management of the company. Its key responsibilities include:

- Ensuring accountability of management
- Protecting the interests of shareholders
- Providing strategic direction
- Monitoring performance and risk

The board acts as a bridge between shareholders and management, ensuring that decisions are made in the best interest of the company.

## Importance of Corporate Governance

Strong corporate governance promotes transparency, accountability, and ethical behavior within organizations. It reduces the likelihood of misconduct and helps build trust with investors and stakeholders.

## Corporate Governance and Fraud Prevention

Corporate governance plays a critical role in preventing fraud. It establishes internal controls, oversight mechanisms, and accountability structures that reduce opportunities for misconduct.

By enforcing checks and balances, organizations can detect irregularities early and respond effectively.
