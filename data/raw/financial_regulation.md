# Financial Regulation

Financial regulation refers to the laws and rules that govern financial institutions and markets.

## Purpose of Financial Regulation

The primary objectives of financial regulation include:

- Maintaining financial stability
- Protecting consumers and investors
- Ensuring transparency in financial markets
- Preventing systemic risk

Regulation helps ensure that financial systems operate fairly and efficiently.

## Key Aspects

Financial regulation typically involves oversight by regulatory bodies, enforcement of compliance standards, and monitoring of financial activities.

## Relationship with Corporate Governance

Financial regulation establishes external rules that organizations must follow, while corporate governance ensures internal compliance with those rules.

Together, they promote accountability and reduce the risk of financial misconduct.
