# Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

Money laundering is the process of disguising illegally obtained money so that it appears legitimate.

## Stages of Money Laundering

1. Placement – Introducing illegal funds into the financial system
2. Layering – Concealing the origin through complex transactions
3. Integration – Reintroducing funds as legitimate assets

## Purpose of AML Regulations

Anti-money laundering (AML) regulations are designed to:

- Detect suspicious financial activity
- Prevent illegal funds from entering the financial system
- Require reporting of suspicious transactions

## Importance

AML frameworks help combat financial crime and maintain the integrity of financial systems.

Organizations are required to implement monitoring systems, reporting mechanisms, and compliance procedures to detect and prevent money laundering.
